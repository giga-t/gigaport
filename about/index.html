Make it

I sketch wireframes and make prototypes. Talking through tactile designs existing in the browser is worth its weight. Design tools only carry you so far; the rest should be realized with a link my team can rally around.

02

Collaborate

Good design is not created in a vacuum but rather in a shared space. It must be facilitated and iterated upon as a team. I aim to include stakeholders in my design process and create a collaborative environment that welcomes and encourages feedback.

03

Build Performant Code

Performance is not a feature; it's a requirement. I focus on optimizing asset delivery, minimizing render-blocking resources, and writing efficient JavaScript to ensure fast loading times and a smooth 60fps interaction experience for every user.

04

Maintain Quality & Scale

I prioritize code health through strict component architecture, proper typing (e.g., TypeScript), and comprehensive unit testing. This approach ensures the codebase remains maintainable, scalable, and resilient as the product grows.

Before I stepped fully into web development, I worked as a virtual assistant. A lot of my early tasks involved handling emails, content, and small technical fixes — long before I knew I’d fall in love with writing real code. Those moments were my first glimpse into how the web actually works.

As I grew more curious, I began studying web development and building projects of my own. From recreating classic games like the Simon Game to experimenting with UI layouts and Tailwind, every project has pushed me to understand not just how things look, but how they function behind the scenes. Now, my learning journey is driven by the excitement of exploring new languages, new technologies, and where the future of the web is heading.

Outside of coding, I enjoy working on personal projects, cooking, learning new skills, and improving the systems around me. I’m driven by a genuine love for building, solving problems, and growing one step at a time.
